Solar System

There are many planetary systems like ours in the universe,
with planets orbiting a host star. Our planetary system is called “the solar system
because we use the word solar to describe things related to our star, after the Latin word for Sun, solis.

Earth

Earth, our home planet, is a world unlike any other. The third planet from the sun,
Earth is the only place in the known universe confirmed to host life. With a radius of 3,959 miles, Earth is the fifth largest planet in our solar system,
and it's the only one known for sure .

Mars

Mars is the fourth planet from the Sun a dusty, cold, desert world with a very thin atmosphere.
Mars is also a dynamic planet with seasons, polar ice caps, canyons, extinct volcanoes,

Jupiter

Jupiter is the fifth planet from our Sun and is, by far,
the largest planet in the solar system – more than twice as massive as all the other planets combined.,

Saturn

Saturn is the sixth planet from the Sun and the second-largest planet in our solar system.
Adorned with thousands of beautiful ringlets, Saturn is unique among the planets.

Mercury

The smallest planet in our solar system and nearest to the Sun,
Mercury is only slightly larger than Earth's Moon.

Neptune

Dark, cold, and whipped by supersonic winds,
ice giant Neptune is the eighth and most distant planet in our solar system.

Uranus

Uranus is the seventh planet from the Sun,
and has the third-largest diameter in our solar system.
It was the first planet found with the aid of a telescope,

Venus

Venus is the second planet from the Sun and is Earth is closest planetary neighbor.
It is one of the four inner, terrestrial (or rocky) planets, and it is often called Earth is twin
because it is similar in size and density.
